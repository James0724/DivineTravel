Great Migration Safaris

A calendar
of two million hooves.

July through October for the river crossings in Masai Mara & Serengeti. December through March for the calving season in the southern Serengeti. Our safari experts help you pick the perfect dates for the experiences you want.

The river crossings.

By late July the front of the herd has reached the Mara River. They mass on the south bank for days, sometimes a week, before a single crossing tips them over the edge. We position you on the north bank at first light. Most years our guests see at least one crossing per stay.

The Itinerary

Three days. Two full game drives.
One Land Cruiser, one guide.

A 3 Days Masai Mara Safari is the perfect escape into Kenya's greatest wildlife reserve — home of the iconic Big Five, rolling savannah plains, dramatic sunsets, and one of the most spectacular wildlife concentrations on the planet. Whether you're witnessing the Great Wildebeest Migration, tracking big cats at sunrise, or enjoying sundowners overlooking endless plains, the Masai Mara promises a safari experience like no other.

01 · Masai Mara
Big cats · Great Migration

Masai Mara National Reserve

Kenya's most famous safari park, and arguably the best destination in all of Africa for big cats and dramatic predator–prey action. The Mara is the northern reach of the Serengeti ecosystem — open, golden, and thick with life.

  • The Great Migration river crossings, July to October
  • Large resident lion prides
  • Excellent leopard & cheetah sightings
  • Massive wildebeest and zebra herds
  • Sunrise hot-air balloon safaris
Best forBig cats & the Migration
02 · Amboseli
Elephants · Kilimanjaro

Amboseli National Park

Known as the Land of Giants — huge elephant herds moving across the dust beneath the snow-capped silhouette of Mount Kilimanjaro. Amboseli is ideal for photographers and families seeking relaxed, immersive encounters.

  • Huge herds of big-tusker elephants
  • Close-up elephant photography
  • Spectacular views of Mount Kilimanjaro
  • Excellent birdlife around the wetlands
  • Accessible, easy year-round game viewing
Best forElephants, photography & families
03 · Tsavo E & W
Wilderness · Big herds

Tsavo East & West

Kenya's vast, untamed wilderness — two adjoining parks that together form one of the largest protected areas on Earth. Combine both for the complete Tsavo experience: raw landscapes and a fraction of the crowds.

Tsavo East
  • Wild, rugged landscapes
  • The famous red elephants
  • Big herds along the Galana River
  • Lion & cheetah · fewer crowds
Tsavo West
  • Volcanic lava flows
  • Crystal-clear Mzima Springs
  • Ngulia Rhino Sanctuary
  • Scenic conservancies & rich birdlife
Best forA raw, untamed safari
04 · Samburu
Rare species · Culture

Samburu National Reserve

A strikingly beautiful, semi-arid reserve in the north — home to the Samburu Special Five, a set of dry-country species you won't find in the southern parks, and deep cultural ties to the Samburu people.

  • Grevy's zebra — the largest, rarest zebra
  • Reticulated giraffe
  • Somali ostrich
  • Beisa oryx
  • Gerenuk — the long-necked "giraffe gazelle"
Best forRare northern wildlife
05 · Ol Pejeta
Rhinos · Conservation

Ol Pejeta Conservancy

A global conservation success story on the Laikipia plateau, and home to the largest population of black rhinos in East Africa — as well as the last two northern white rhinos on Earth.

  • Largest black rhino population in East Africa
  • The last two northern white rhinos on Earth
  • Among Kenya's highest predator densities
  • Conservancy-level, hands-on experiences
Best forRhinos & luxury conservation

Best time to visit

A year-round
destination.

Kenya is good all year — the best months simply depend on what you want to see. Here's the short version; we'll tune your dates around the wildlife you're chasing.

Dry season · June – October

Peak game viewing.

  • Best wildlife viewing overall
  • Peak of the Great Migration in the Mara
  • Clear skies and easy, open game viewing
  • Thinner vegetation concentrates animals at water
Green season · November – May

Lush, quiet & better value.

  • Lower prices and fewer vehicles
  • Lush, photogenic green landscapes
  • Excellent birdwatching as migrants arrive
  • Calving season in February — predator action

Gorilla trekking regions

Where you'll
trek.

Two national parks in Uganda's far south-west protect the mountain gorillas — one vast and famous, one small and scenic. We secure permits in whichever suits your trip.

01 · Bwindi
19+ habituated families · 4 sectors

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

The heart of Uganda gorilla trekking — a dense, ancient rainforest holding more than nineteen habituated families across four trekking sectors. The name is not marketing: the terrain is steep and tangled, and the reward is an hour you will never forget.

Northern sectors
  • Buhoma — the original, most established sector
  • Ruhija — high-altitude, fewer crowds
Southern sectors
  • Rushaga — the most gorilla families
  • Nkuringo — dramatic, demanding descents
Best forChoice & permit availability
02 · Mgahinga
The Nyakagezi family · Golden monkeys

Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

Smaller and strikingly scenic, set against the Virunga volcanoes. Mgahinga is home to the single habituated Nyakagezi family and far fewer visitors — and it pairs gorilla trekking with golden monkey tracking and volcano hikes.

  • The habituated Nyakagezi gorilla family
  • Golden monkey trekking
  • Dramatic Virunga volcano backdrop
  • Quiet trails and few other groups
Best forScenery & solitude
What happens on a trek

A morning in
the forest.

Every trek follows the same rhythm — an early briefing, a walk of anywhere from one to five hours, and a single, strictly protected hour with the family once you find them.

Step 01
7:00 AM

Briefing

Rangers explain the trekking rules and assign you to a gorilla family at the park headquarters.

Step 02
8:00 AM

Start trek

You set off into the rainforest with guides and trackers who are already locating the family.

Step 03
1–5 hrs

The search

Trekking time depends on where the gorillas have moved — terrain includes hills, dense growth and streams.

Step 04
One hour

With the gorillas

Observe them feeding, nesting, playing and grooming — exactly sixty protected minutes, no chasing.

Step 05
Certificate

Awarded at the end

You return with a trekking certificate — and one of the most emotional wildlife experiences of your life.
